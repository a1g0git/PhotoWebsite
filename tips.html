Want to take great photos of your roses?

Watch the slideshow video below for examples of how these tips can help. Merv's photos of roses are prize-winning! Check out Merv's tips.

What kind of camera do you have? Click the button for your type to see the tips

Set Your Camera

  • Set Camera to Aperture Mode or "A" for Aperture Priority (The camera's brain will automatically adjust "T" or shutter speed.)
  • Choose "Landscape" setting
  • Choose "Landscape" Aperture setting (F Stop) of at least 16 to maximize sharpness of entire blossom.
  • Set your camera's ISO to 400 or choose "variable" to allow camera to set ISO for each shot

Hold it steady!

  • Hold the Camera Still!
  • Hold the camera in both hands.
  • Lock your arms against your chest.
  • For best results, USE A TRIPOD!!! Put camera on tripod (!!!) to get a sharp picture.

Choose your rose

  • Select rose to photograph as if you were a rose show judge.
  • Would you take this rose to a rose show? The photo can be no better than the chosen subject.
  • Is the foliage quality equal to that of the blossom?
  • Think: â€œI will submit this photo in the ? contest category.
  • What ribbon should I expect for this rose?

Control what else is in the picture

  • Will I photograph blossom with foliage or blossom alone?
  • Will the background detract from the beauty of the rose?
  • Is the background close to or distant from the rose?
  • Can you move for a better background shot?
  • Add a background screen to your photo equipment?
  • If it's in a vase, does the vase look good?

Consider the Light

  • What time is it? (Where is the sun?)
  • Is the sun shining on my rose?
  • Is my rose in the shade?
  • Is there a shadow on part of the rose?

Frame the Shot!

  • Consider angle of camera to blossom.
  • Point your camera at your rose.
  • Now, zoom in or move closer to fill the frame.
  • Use the "Rule of Thirds" as a guide: no bullseye!
    • Rule of Thirds Definition: think tic-tac-toe
    • Locate point of interest near where the lines intersect.
    • Focus on the Right Spot
    • Divide the rose into (vertical) thirds.
    • Focus on the division line nearest to the camera.
    • 1/3 of the photo nearest the camera will be in focus.
    • 2/3 of the photo farthest from camera will be in focus.

Check your Equipment!

  • Camera and battery woking?
  • Lens
  • Filter: Polarizer
  • Flash
  • Tripod AND Tripod Head
